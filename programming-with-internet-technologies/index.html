I did two tours while I was onboard the U.S.S. John C Stennis(CVN-74)aircraft carrier.

My first cruise took me around the world. The ship left Norfolk, VA in Jan 1998 to do a homeport change to San Diego in Aug 1998 that took me around the world. From Norfolk the ship headed through the Strait of Gilbrator, the Suez Canal, and Strait of Hormuz to enter the Persian Gulf. While in the Persian Gulf we visited United Arabic Emirates, Jebel Ali, Dubai, Bahrain. After leaving the Persian Gulf we to Fremantle and Perth, Austrilia and Holbart, Tazmania. Then we headed to Hawai'i for a few days then ported in San Diego.

My second cruise was a West Pac cruise. The ship was deployed from San Diego in Feb 2000 - August 2000. During this cruise we went to United Arabic Emirates, Jebel Ali, Dubai, Bahrain, Malaysia, Hong Kong, Hawai'i, South Korea, Fremantle and Perth, Austrilia and Holbart, Tazmania.

Between cruises, the ship has to do work ups to get the ship prepared to deploy. During the work ups the ship travelled to Nova Scotia, Saint Marteen and Vancouver, B.C. for port visits.

My job was navigation known as a Quarter Master. I was discharged honorably as a Quarter Master Third Class Petty Officer(QM3 SW/AW), Enlisted Surface Warfare Specialist (ESWS), Enlisted Aviation Warfare Specialist (EAWS).
